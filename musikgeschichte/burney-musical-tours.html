About

Dr. Charles Burney (1726–1814), English music historian, undertook two tours of Europe to gather material for his General History of Music (1776–1789). Armed with letters of introduction, he visited opera houses, conservatories, and royal courts — interviewing the greatest composers and performers of his age.

His two published travel diaries remain among the most vivid documents of 18th-century musical life.

Key Figures Met

  • Padre Martini · Bologna — Europe's foremost music historian; Burney examined his library of 17,000 manuscripts
  • Farinelli · Bologna — The legendary castrato, in comfortable retirement
  • C.P.E. Bach · Hamburg — Director of music for Hamburg's churches; demonstrated his keyboard style
  • Frederick the Great · Potsdam — Monarch and serious flautist; Burney heard him perform
  • Johann Joachim Quantz · Potsdam — Frederick's flute teacher and court composer
  • Christoph Willibald Gluck · Vienna — Opera reformer; Burney called him "a great and powerful genius"
  • Pietro Metastasio · Vienna — The ageing but still revered master of opera seria libretti
  • Joseph Haydn · Eszterháza — Burney made a special detour to hear Haydn conduct his symphonies
  • Leopold Mozart · Salzburg — Father of the prodigy; showed Burney Wolfgang's compositions
  • Nicola Jommelli · Naples — Leading Neapolitan opera composer, recently returned from Stuttgart
